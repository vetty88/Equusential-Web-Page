Case Studies

Our Loyal Clients

Duke

Duke went from despair to domination! Duke had suffered a serious floating accident and suspected stringhalt. He was a mess! It has been a long journey of dedication, care and commitment to get him to blue-ribbon winning condition. Duke is a long-term client of Equusential, owned by our formerly sponsored rider and long-term client Krystel Handley! He was an original case study! To learn more about Duke's journey please see his blog post!

Squirrel

Squirrel suffered significant back pain and scarring from poor saddle fit. Her back is now healthy and the scars gone! Yvette's own "heart horse" Squirrel is the reason that Equusential began, Yvette learnt massage to help Squirrel's back pain. Squirrel was suffering back pain and muscle damage/ scarring from an ill-fitting saddle. Squirrel's journey can be found on our blog. Also check out our social media as she is a familiar face!

Rage

Rage had long-term lameness of unknown origin. He is now ridden regularly and doing so much better! Rage is Krystel's number #1 horse. In recent times Krystel has worked diligently with the massage and rehabilitation pogram that Yvette set, with outstanding results! This program focused particularly on strengthening his deep back and core muscles. To date he is now sound and well! You will see this golden boy feature regularly on our Facebook page!

Bobby

Bobby suffered extreme muscular atrophy and exhibited typical signs of stringhalt/shivers syndrome. He was depressed and lost appetite. EMT and rehabilitation has helped Bobby to grow his muscle back and be competitively sound! Bobby is Barb's horse. He became an Equusential client in winter 2020 when Barb had taken him on. Barb worked diligently, patiently and progressively from day dot to rehabilitate Bobby. This approach was multi-faceted and drew on Barb's years of experience and knowledge caring for and training horses. Regular massages and rehabilitation exercises were key to Bobby's recovery. Bobby became sound, happy and competitive!

KitKat

KitKat was lame in her hind leg in training and jumping. She benefited from a dedicated massage and rehab program and is now competitive and sound again! KitKat became an Equusential client when she began pulling up lame in the hind leg, especially after jumping. At the time she was training for Stockman's Challenges. KitKat's owner, Kristen, saw that she was unsound and made the decision to pull her from competition and focus on rehabilitation. Following a massage and rehabilitation program set by Yvette that included polework and gradual building of tone and fitness from the 'ground-up' Kristen is now enjoying a sound and competitive KitKat who has resumed jumping with no soundess concerns!
